<p>To remove a <em>shortcode</em>, simply leave the field to be empty.</p>
<h3>Usage</h3>
<h4>As <abbr title="Hyper Text Markup Language">HTML</abbr></h4>
<pre><code class="html">&lt;a href="`{{url.page:about}}`"&gt;link text&lt;/a&gt;</code></pre>
<h4>As Markdown</h4>
<pre><code class="markdown">[link text](`{{url.page:about}}`)</code></pre>
<h3>Escaping a Shortcode</h3>
<p>Wrap your <em>shortcode</em> with backtick characters to prevent code parsing:</p>
<pre><code class="markdown">Parse `{{this}}` pattern, but not ``{{this}}``</code></pre>
<h3>Advanced</h3>
<p>You can pass some variables inside a <em>shortcode</em> pattern via <code>&#37;s</code> symbol. For example:</p>
<table class="table-bordered table-full-width">
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>Key</th>
      <th>Value</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
  <tr>
    <td><code>`{{div class:&#37;s id:&#37;s}}`</code></td>
    <td><code>&lt;div class="$1" id="$2"&gt;</code></td>
  </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
<p>So, writing <code>`{{div class:foo id:bar}}`</code> in the page composer will produce <code>&lt;div class="foo" id="bar"&gt;</code> in the output.</p>
